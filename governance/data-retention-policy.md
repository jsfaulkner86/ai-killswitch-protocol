# AI Kill Switch Protocol — Data Retention Policy
**The Faulkner Group | Version 1.0.0 | 2026-04-06**

> Classification: RESTRICTED — Clinical Safety Critical  
> Regulatory Basis: HIPAA 45 CFR 164.316(b)(2), FDA 21 CFR Part 11.10(e), CMS Conditions of Participation

---

## Purpose

This policy defines retention periods, storage requirements, and disposition procedures
for all records generated by the AI Kill Switch Protocol, including audit logs,
incident reports, override justifications, and reinstatement records.

---

## Retention Schedule

| Record Type | Minimum Retention | Storage Requirement | Disposition |
|---|---|---|---|
| Kill switch halt event audit logs | 6 years | WORM-compliant, encrypted | Secure delete + certificate |
| Override justification records | 6 years | WORM-compliant, encrypted | Secure delete + certificate |
| Reinstatement checklists | 6 years | WORM-compliant, encrypted | Secure delete + certificate |
| Incident response reports | 6 years | WORM-compliant, encrypted | Secure delete + certificate |
| PHI breach investigation records | 6 years from date of breach | WORM-compliant, encrypted | Legal hold may extend |
| Confidence calibration logs | 3 years | Standard encrypted storage | Secure delete |
| Drift detection records | 3 years | Standard encrypted storage | Secure delete |
| Circuit breaker state logs | 2 years | Standard encrypted storage | Secure delete |
| Kill switch test drill logs | 3 years | Standard encrypted storage | Secure delete |
| Agent/twin registry versions | Lifetime of agent + 6 years | Version-controlled, encrypted | Secure delete |

---

## Storage Requirements

All records subject to this policy must be stored with:

1. **Encryption at rest**: AES-256 or equivalent
2. **Encryption in transit**: TLS 1.3
3. **WORM compliance** (for audit logs and incident records): AWS S3 Object Lock, Azure Immutable Blob Storage, or equivalent
4. **Access control**: Role-based, per `/governance/roles-and-permissions.yaml`
5. **Tamper evidence**: SHA-256 hash per record, chain hash for audit logs
6. **Geographic redundancy**: Minimum 2 geographically separate copies

---

## Legal Hold

When litigation, regulatory investigation, or judicial hold is in place:

- All retention clocks are **suspended** for affected records
- Records subject to hold must be flagged in the audit system
- Legal Counsel must authorize any disposition of held records
- Hold status must be reviewed every 90 days

---

## Disposition Procedure

1. Confirm retention period has elapsed and no legal hold is active
2. Obtain written approval from Compliance Officer
3. Execute secure deletion (NIST 800-88 compliant)
4. Generate and retain disposition certificate for 3 years
